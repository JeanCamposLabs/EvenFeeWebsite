The problem

Measured wrong once. You pay on every unit.

Amazon sets FBA fulfillment and storage fees from each product's recorded size and weight. A unit measured even slightly too large lands in a higher size tier — where the fee is higher on every single unit.

Fee per unit ↑ larger size tier →
Small
standard Large

large

A few millimetres or grams can bump a unit up a tier — then the higher fee hits every unit you ship and store, until it's corrected.

FAQ

Questions, answered honestly.

Is EvenFee affiliated with Amazon?

No. EvenFee is an independent service and is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Amazon. We integrate with Amazon's official Selling Partner API (SP-API) only with your authorization.

What data does EvenFee access?

Only your own seller account's FBA fee, inventory, shipment, and financial data, accessed via Amazon's SP-API. We never access buyer names, addresses, or any buyer personal data. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

How does EvenFee get paid?

A small monthly subscription plus a success fee charged only on amounts we actually help you recover. If there's no recovery, there's no success fee.

Can I revoke EvenFee's access?

Yes — at any time, directly from your Amazon Seller Central account. When you revoke access, we stop processing your data and delete it, except where limited records must be kept to meet legal obligations.

Do you guarantee a recovery amount?

No. Outcomes depend on your specific account and Amazon's review of each claim. Because our success fee only applies to amounts that are actually recovered, our incentives stay aligned with yours.

Where is EvenFee based?

EvenFee is operated by an independent sole proprietor based in Portugal (EU), and follows GDPR for any personal data it processes.
