FBA fee reimbursements: what sellers should know

Some FBA fee overcharges can be recovered — if you find them, substantiate them, and file in time. Here's a plain-English overview.

What can be reimbursed

Amazon operates reimbursement processes for a range of FBA discrepancies. Fee overcharges caused by incorrect dimension or weight records are one category sellers can pursue: if a product was placed in the wrong size tier, the difference billed while that error was in place may be recoverable.

The general process

  1. Identify the discrepancy. Compare Amazon's recorded dimensions and the assigned size tier against the product's true, measured size.
  2. Get it corrected. Request a re-measurement so the size tier — and your future fees — are set correctly.
  3. File a claim. Submit the overcharge through Amazon's standard channels with clear supporting detail, and track the outcome.

Why timing matters

Amazon generally limits how far back fee claims can be made. The longer an error sits unnoticed, the more of the overcharge you may no longer be able to reclaim. Reviewing your fees regularly — or monitoring continuously — protects the recoverable window before it closes.

Keep good records

Document a product's true dimensions and weight, the values Amazon had on record, and the dates involved. Clear, organised evidence makes a claim easier to substantiate and quicker to resolve.

How EvenFee helps

EvenFee audits your fees, helps prepare and file claims, and monitors for new measurement errors — and our success fee applies only to amounts actually recovered. Outcomes always depend on Amazon's review of each individual claim.
